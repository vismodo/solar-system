Lured by Gravity



Satellites are celestial bodies that share a orbital path with a larger or primary body. These are typically smaller in size in comparison to its counterpart and does not meet fully with the criteria that would constitute it as a planet or primary object. Any natural object that orbits a primary object, but does not meet the qualifications of a planet, dwarf planet, or satellite is considered a Moon. These objects are typically irregular in shape and are thought to have been asteroids at one time becoming caught in the gravitational pull of a "parent" object.

Notable Moons

  1. Earth
    • Moon or Luna - serves an important part of our planets oceanic systems determining their tides and currents.
  2. Mars
    • Deimos - has a mean radius of 6.2 kms(3.9 miles) and takes 30.3 hours to orbit
    • Phobos - is larger and closer of the two natural sattelites of the planet Mars.
  3. Jupiter
    • Io - is the closest satellite to Jupiter and the second smallest of the four Galilean Moons. Io has over 400 active volcanoes on its surface.
    • Europa - the smallest of Jupiter's Galilean Moons, it has an ocean which flows beneath its surface.
  4. Saturn
    • Mimas - nicknamed by some as the "Death Star", it's surface consist primarily of water ice. It is the smallest known body in space to be rounded by its own gravitation.
    • Enceladus - Beneath its surface is an ocean that ejects water vapor into the atmosphere. This water vapor is what contributes to Saturn's "E-Ring".
    • Titan - A golden haze surrounds this interesting satellite given to its nitrogen-rich atmosphere.
    • Iapetus - Is the last of Saturn's satellites. It is three quarters ice and one quarter rock.
  5. Neptune
    • Triton - of Neptune's 13 moons, only Triton qualified as a satellite. Triton has a retrograde rotation where it rotates in the opposite direction of its parent planet.
  6. Pluto
    • Charon - The dwarf planet Pluto has five moons In order of distance from Pluto, they are Charon, Styx, Nix, Kerberos, and Hydra.
